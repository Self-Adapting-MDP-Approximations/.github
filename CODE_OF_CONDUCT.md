# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

Self-Adapting MDP Approximations is intended to be a welcoming, respectful, and intellectually serious space for researchers, students, and practitioners. We want the project to support open scholarly discussion while maintaining a professional and constructive environment.

## Expected Behavior

Participants in this community are expected to:

- be respectful in discussions, issues, and pull requests,
- engage ideas critically without attacking people,
- assume good faith and communicate with clarity,
- welcome questions from learners and researchers at different stages,
- provide feedback in a constructive and professional manner.

## Unacceptable Behavior

The following kinds of behavior are not acceptable:

- harassment, intimidation, or personal attacks,
- discriminatory or abusive language,
- dismissive, hostile, or deliberately inflammatory conduct,
- bad-faith disruption of discussions or collaborative work,
- publishing private or sensitive information without permission.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to interactions in the project's repositories, issue trackers, discussions, pull requests, and other public spaces associated with the project.

## Enforcement

Project maintainers may remove, edit, or moderate comments, contributions, discussions, or other interactions that are inconsistent with this Code of Conduct. They may also take additional action when necessary to protect the health of the community.

## Reporting

If you encounter behavior that violates this Code of Conduct, please report it privately to the maintainers through an agreed project contact channel once the organization is established.

## Final Note

This project aims to model the best parts of a healthy research community: rigor, curiosity, generosity, and respect.
